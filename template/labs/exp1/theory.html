Earthquake Time Series Analyser

Theory

Introduction to Seismic Signals

Seismic signals represent ground motion generated by natural processes (earthquakes, volcanic activity) or artificial sources (explosions, controlled vibrators). These signals contain crucial information about Earth materials, fault behavior, and wave propagation. Seismic records typically consist of three components (Z, N-S, E-W) captured by sensitive seismometers.

Time-Domain Representation

In the time domain, the seismic trace is plotted as amplitude versus time. This helps identify:

  • P-wave, S-wave, and Surface-wave arrivals
  • Signal duration and waveform shape
  • Ground motion amplitude and energy release pattern
  • Onset of events and noise bursts

Time-domain analysis is essential for event detection, travel-time picking, and first-motion studies.

Frequency-Domain Analysis

Time-domain plots do not describe how energy varies across frequencies. Frequency-domain tools, especially the Fast Fourier Transform (FFT), reveal dominant frequency bands and spectral content. This helps in:

  • Distinguishing seismic events from background noise
  • Identifying resonant frequencies of the Earth or structures
  • Characterizing wave types and soil/rock behavior

Spectrogram Analysis

A spectrogram displays how signal frequency content evolves over time using the Short-Time Fourier Transform (STFT). It provides a time-frequency-amplitude representation and is useful for:

  • Tracking wave arrivals and phase conversions
  • Visualizing transient seismic events
  • Identifying frequency build-up during earthquakes
  • Noise pattern recognition (cultural, wind, traffic, instrument noise)

Digital Filtering

Seismic data can include environmental and instrument noise. Digital filters help clean and enhance signals by isolating specific frequency ranges:

  • Low-Pass Filter: Retains low frequencies, removes high-frequency noise
  • High-Pass Filter: Retains high frequencies, removes long-period drifts
  • Band-Pass Filter: Preserves a defined frequency band useful for body-wave studies

Filtering improves clarity of seismic phases and enhances interpretation of event characteristics.
